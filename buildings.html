Sycamore Square

Sycamore Square is a landmark building, providing thirty-two quality office spaces and retail shops right at the center of Fairhaven. This prominent location offers convenient access for offices and marketing opportunities for restaurants and retails.

Built in 1890, Sycamore Square was originally known as the Mason Block. It was renamed the Market Place in the 1970s. The year 1994 saw a major interior renovation that included improvements to the building layout as well as the reintroduction of much detailed woodwork. Despite these changes, Sycamore Square retains the rugged brick exterior, the intricate soffit designs, and the broad arched windows that have always distinguished this building.

12th Street Village

12th Street Village is a commercial complex with twenty-four office and retail spaces. Featuring a multi-functional outdoor courtyard and a designated parking lot, 12th Street provides valuable exposure for the shops on the lower floor. Businesses on the upper floor enjoy quiet and convenient workspaces as well as unobstructed views of Bellingham Bay and the San Juan Islands.

The exterior of 12th Street Village exemplifies the old world architecture for which Fairhaven is known. Supported by timber braces, deep eaves drape over rich brick walls. Dark trim surrounds the broad arched windows, and exposed wood beams and columns frame the open air walkways. With these quintessential features, 12th Street Village is the paragon of Old Fairhaven charm.

Current tenants at 12th Street Village include specialty shops, real estate firms, and medical professionals.

Stanellos

With six retail store fronts and private offices, Stanellos (also known as 12th Street Square) boasts a spacious layout and a convenient location. Just off 12th Street and Donavan Ave, Stanellos provides quality workspaces to help your company succeed.

The detailed roofline and adobe colored walls of the Stanellos building are remaniscint of Italian design. Other architectural features include prominent corner columns, decorative window trim, and diamond motifs.

Currently, Stanellos tenants include a physical therapy center, a hair studio, and a financial advisory office.
